About Paul

A Career Dedicated to Justice

Paul Dedinsky is a 25-year veteran prosecutor who has worked in the criminal justice system for over thirty years. Paul received his law degree from the University of Wisconsin–Madison Law School in 1993, and his Ph.D. in Education and Leadership in 2012.

Governor Walker first appointed Paul as Chief Legal Counsel to the Wisconsin Department of Agriculture, Trade, and Consumer Protection (DATCP) from 2017–2018. Governor Walker then appointed Judge Paul Dedinsky (2019–2020). With experience as a judge, as well as a sexual assault and domestic violence prosecutor who worked with hundreds upon hundreds of victims, Paul is acutely aware of the toll of the system upon victims of crime.

Paul Dedinsky has taught law as well as business law and ethics to undergrads and graduate-level students at universities like Marquette and Cardinal Stritch. Paul is married to Lisa, and the couple raised three children — Abby, Charlie, and Natalia — in Delafield, Wisconsin.
